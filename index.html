About me

I enjoy transforming real-world data into applications that help people.

I am currently Lead Machine Learning Scientist at Limbic, where I use AI to build psychotherapy for the 21st century.

I strongly believe that AI can be a positive transformational force in the world. That's why I devote much of my time to making sure that AI is deployed responsibly and actually helps people improve their wellbeing.

Previously, I was an academic in Neuroscience and Experimental Psychology at the University of Oxford. First as a PhD student at Wolfson College, then as a Junior Research Fellow at St John's College. My primary interest was in using Reinforcement Learning to understand how people select and pursue conflicting goals. This paper best summarises my interests.

In my spare time I enjoy writing fiction and I am an alumnus of the 7-month Writing a Novel course at Faber Academy.
